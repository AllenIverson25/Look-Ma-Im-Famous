Early Life

Born near Almonte, Ontario on November 6, 1861, James was the eldest son of Scottish immigrants John and Margaret Naismith. Jim headed for McGill University’s gymnasium where he soon began participating in the gymnastic and rugby program. By his junior year, he was winning the university’s highest honours for his athletic involvement.

Major Acomplishment

Inventing basketball: In 1891, Naismith invented basketball as an indoor alternative to football that would be less likely to cause injuries. He created the game to keep athletes active during the winter. Writing the first basketball rulebook: Naismith wrote the original 13 rules of basketball.

Significant Contributions

Keeping students occupied Naismith was tasked with keeping a group of rowdy 14-year-old boys at the YMCA in Springfield, Massachusetts occupied during a harsh New England winter. Creating an indoor game The students had too much energy to burn off, but it was too cold to play football or baseball, and too dangerous to play those sports in the gym.

Notable Challenges

Creating an indoor game Naismith was tasked with creating a game that could be played indoors during the winter to keep students active.
