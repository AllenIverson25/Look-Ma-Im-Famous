Early Life

Born near Almonte, Ontario on November 6, 1861, James was the eldest son of Scottish immigrants John and Margaret Naismith. Jim headed for McGill University’s gymnasium where he soon began participating in the gymnastic and rugby program. By his junior year, he was winning the university’s highest honours for his athletic involvement. While working for the YMCA, James was given the task of introducing a new indoor game in 1891, with two main objectives – “make it fair for all players, and free of rough play”.

Major Acomplishments

Inventing basketball: In 1891, Naismith invented basketball as an indoor alternative to football that would be less likely to cause injuries. He created the game to keep athletes active during the winter.
Writing the first basketball rulebook: Naismith wrote the original 13 rules of basketball.
Establishing the University of Kansas basketball program: Naismith founded the basketball program at the University of Kansas.

Significant Contributions

Keeping students occupied Naismith was tasked with keeping a group of rowdy 14-year-old boys at the YMCA in Springfield, Massachusetts occupied during a harsh New England winter. Creating an indoor game The students had too much energy to burn off, but it was too cold to play football or baseball, and too dangerous to play those sports in the gym.

Notable Challenges

Creating an indoor game Naismith was tasked with creating a game that could be played indoors during the winter to keep students active. Keeping it safe Naismith needed to create a game that was safe to play indoors, avoiding the risk of injuries that could occur from football, soccer, or rugby. Making it interesting Naismith needed to create a game that was easy to learn, but complex enough to be interesting.
